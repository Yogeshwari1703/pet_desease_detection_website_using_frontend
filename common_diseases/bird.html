Birds Disease Encyclopedia

Comprehensive guide to common bird illnesses and treatments

Psittacosis (Parrot Fever) Severe
Birds

Overview

Zoonotic disease caused by Chlamydophila bacteria affecting over 400 bird species. Also called Parrot Fever, it can infect humans and other animals.

Symptoms

  • Swollen, puffy eyes
  • Anorexia and lethargy
  • Weight loss
  • Respiratory distress

Treatment

Doxycycline is the antibiotic of choice. Improve hygiene, disinfect habitats, and isolate infected birds. Avoid contact with wild birds.

Proventricular Dilatation Disease (PDD) Severe
Birds

Overview

Caused by Avian Bornavirus affecting nerves to the gastrointestinal tract, especially the proventriculus. Common in parrots, macaws, and African greys.

Symptoms

  • Ataxia and depression
  • Weakness
  • Lack of appetite
  • Weight loss
  • Undigested food in droppings

Treatment

No specific treatment available. Supportive care with anti-inflammatory drugs. Isolate affected birds from healthy ones.

Polyomavirus Critical
Birds

Overview

Fatal disease primarily affecting young parrots. Adults often develop immunity. Spread through direct contact with infected birds.

Symptoms

  • Loss of appetite
  • Diarrhea and vomiting
  • Feather abnormalities
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Hemorrhage under skin

Treatment

No specific treatment. Prevention through strict hygiene, disinfection, and vaccination. Often fatal in young birds.

Psittacine Beak and Feather Disease (PBFD) Critical
Birds

Overview

Contagious viral disease caused by circovirus affecting beak, feathers, and immune system. Affects over 40 bird species.

Symptoms

  • Depression and regurgitation
  • Abnormal feather development
  • Fragile, deformed feathers
  • Irregular beak growth
  • Mucus discharge

Treatment

No cure. Supportive care includes nutritional support, beak trimming, and treating secondary infections. Quarantine new birds. Vaccine available in Australia.

Candidiasis Moderate
Birds

Overview

Fungal infection caused by Candida albicans, normally present but overgrows due to antibiotics, poor sanitation, or vitamin A deficiency.

Symptoms

  • Fluffed feathers
  • Lack of appetite
  • Vomiting and regurgitation
  • Crop impaction
  • White plaques in mouth

Treatment

Antifungal medications (nystatin, ketoconazole, etc.). Improve hygiene, balanced diet, and avoid unnecessary antibiotics. Amphotericin B for skin/oral infections.

Respiratory Tract Infection (RTI) Severe
Birds

Overview

Common condition affecting lungs, sinuses, and eyes. Caused by bacteria (Psittacosis, Mycoplasma), viruses (Avian Influenza), fungi (Aspergillosis), or parasites.

Symptoms

  • Sneezing and coughing
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Nasal discharge
  • Dehydration
  • Lethargy

Treatment

Depends on cause (antibiotics, antifungals, etc.). Early diagnosis crucial. Vaccines available for some pathogens. Improve nutrition and habitat conditions.

Feather Plucking Moderate
Birds

Overview

Behavioral or medical condition where birds pluck their own feathers. Causes include infections, parasites, stress, loneliness, or nutritional deficiencies.

Symptoms

  • Bald patches
  • Damaged feathers
  • Skin irritation
  • Behavioral changes

Treatment

Address underlying causes. Reduce stress, provide companionship, balanced diet, and adequate space. Medical treatment if infection or parasites present.

Common Eye Problems Moderate
Birds

Overview

Includes conjunctivitis, uveitis, cataracts, and conditions from infections (bacterial, viral, fungal) or nutritional deficiencies (especially vitamin A).

Symptoms

  • Redness and swelling
  • Excessive tearing
  • Discharge or crusting
  • Vision problems
  • Rubbing eyes

Treatment

Antibiotic eye drops (e.g., Chloramphenicol). Identify and treat underlying cause. Vaccination available for some viral causes like Fowl Pox.

Avian Influenza (Bird Flu) Critical
Birds

Overview

Highly contagious viral disease (H5N1 strain is zoonotic). Causes severe respiratory symptoms and high mortality in birds. Can infect humans.

Symptoms

  • Respiratory distress
  • Reduced egg production
  • Swelling of head/face
  • Sudden death
  • Neurological signs

Treatment

No effective treatment. Prevention through vaccination and strict biosecurity. Isolate and report suspected cases immediately.

Gout Severe
Birds

Overview

Result of kidney dysfunction causing uric acid buildup. Two types: visceral (organs) and articular (joints). Caused by diet, renal failure, or toxins.

Symptoms

  • Swollen joints (articular)
  • Lethargy
  • Loss of appetite
  • Difficulty moving
  • White chalky deposits

Treatment

Address underlying kidney issues. Dietary changes (reduce protein), increase hydration. Pain management. Often indicates serious renal disease.
