Buffalo Disease Encyclopedia

Comprehensive guide to common buffalo illnesses and treatments

Foot-and-Mouth Disease Severe
Buffalo

Overview

A severe and highly contagious infection caused by the Aphto-virus (Picornaviridae family) that affects cloven-hoofed animals. Spreads through aerosol transmission, direct contact, and contaminated objects.

Symptoms

  • Painful vesicles on lips, tongue, gums, nostrils, and feet
  • Excessive salivation and depression
  • Anorexia and lameness
  • Fever and decreased milk production

Treatment

Control strategies include vaccination and zoosanitary measures. Eradication policies aim to eliminate virus presence, while management focuses on reducing disease effects.

Rotavirus Infection Severe
Buffalo Calves

Overview

Caused by Rotaviruses (Reoviridae family) with high environmental resistance. Primarily affects calves 1-3 weeks old through oral-fecal contamination.

Symptoms

  • Rapid onset of anorexia and depression
  • Fever and severe dehydration
  • Watery or pasty diarrhea (sometimes with blood)
  • Worst diarrhea after 7-8 days

Treatment

House calves in disinfected cages for 15-20 days. High-quality colostrum is crucial. Vaccinate pregnant females for passive immunization of calves.

Bovine Brucellosis Severe
Buffalo

Overview

Also known as Bang's disease or contagious abortion. Primarily a reproductive disease caused by Brucella abortus that affects pregnant females.

Symptoms

  • Abortion after six months of pregnancy
  • Retained placenta and catarrhal metritis
  • Synovitis and joint swelling
  • Impaired fertility

Treatment

Requires prolonged, aggressive treatment with risks of relapse. Control measures focus on preventing spread to humans through the food chain.

Mastitis Moderate
Buffalo

Overview

Economically significant udder inflammation caused by bacteria (Staphylococcus, E. coli), fungi, or Mycoplasma. Spread through poor hygiene, milking practices, or flies.

Symptoms

  • Swollen, hot, painful udder
  • Milk changes (color, consistency)
  • Blood or clots in milk
  • Reduced milk yield

Treatment

Maintain hygiene, isolate infected animals, apply ice packs, milk out infected milk, and use antibiotics. Control flies and disinfect equipment.

Tuberculosis Severe
Buffalo

Overview

Chronic disease caused by Mycobacterium bovis, lasting months to years. Spread through exhaled air, secretions, and excretions from infected animals.

Symptoms

  • Low-grade fever and appetite loss
  • Emaciation and weakness
  • Chronic cough and pneumonia
  • Swollen lymph nodes (especially supramammary)
  • Dry skin and difficulty breathing

Treatment

No standard treatment due to expense and duration. Limited therapeutic protocols for high-value animals only.
