Dog Skin Disease Encyclopedia

Comprehensive guide to common canine skin conditions and treatments

Acral Lick Dermatitis Moderate
Dog

Overview

A skin condition caused by compulsive licking or chewing of the same area, typically on paws or limbs. Also called lick granulomas, these lesions resist healing and may become infected.

Symptoms

  • Persistent licking/chewing of specific areas
  • Raised, thickened skin lesions
  • Possible infection and scarring
  • Compulsive behavior pattern

Treatment

Identify and treat underlying cause, use anti-pruritics and antibiotics if infected. E-collar to prevent licking. Behavior modification for compulsive cases.

Acute Moist Dermatitis Moderate
Dog

Overview

Commonly called "hot spots," these are red, raw areas that develop suddenly from excessive licking/chewing. Often caused by underlying allergies or parasites.

Symptoms

  • Sudden appearance of red, moist lesions
  • Hair loss in affected area
  • Possible bacterial infection
  • Intense itching and discomfort

Treatment

Shave hair around area, clean thoroughly. Use topical medications, antibiotics if infected. E-collar to prevent further trauma. Treat underlying cause.

Canine Atopic Dermatitis Severe
Dog

Overview

Inherited hypersensitivity reaction causing extreme itching. Often no specific allergen identified. Typically appears between 6 months and 3 years of age.

Symptoms

  • Intense itching (scratching, chewing, rubbing)
  • Hair loss and skin irritation
  • Secondary skin infections
  • Worsened by fleas/parasites

Treatment

Anti-pruritics, antihistamines, steroids. Allergy testing may lead to immunotherapy. Often requires lifelong management of flare-ups.

Ear Infections Moderate
Dog

Overview

Caused by yeast or bacteria infecting the ear canal. May occur alone or with other skin conditions like allergies or ear mites.

Symptoms

  • Red, itchy, swollen ear canal
  • Discharge and odor
  • Head shaking or tilting
  • Pain when touched

Treatment

Ear cleaning and topical medications. Oral/injectable meds for severe cases. Chronic cases may cause permanent damage.

Fleas Moderate
Dog

Overview

Common external parasites causing intense itching. Some dogs develop Flea Allergy Dermatitis (hypersensitivity to flea saliva).

Symptoms

  • Intense scratching and chewing
  • Flea dirt (black specks) on skin
  • Hair loss and skin irritation
  • Possible anemia in severe cases

Treatment

Use veterinarian-recommended flea control products. Treat environment. Severe cases may need additional medications.

Folliculitis Moderate
Dog

Overview

Inflammation of hair follicles, most commonly caused by bacterial infection (Staphylococcus). May occur alone or with other skin problems.

Symptoms

  • Bumps around hair follicles
  • Circular areas of crusting
  • Itching or pain
  • Possible secondary infections

Treatment

Depends on cause - may include antibiotics, antifungals, or antipruritics. Medicated shampoos often helpful.

Food Allergies Moderate
Dog

Overview

Allergic reaction to specific food ingredients (commonly beef, dairy, wheat, chicken, egg, or soy). Causes skin and sometimes GI symptoms.

Symptoms

  • Itching, redness, flakiness
  • Hair loss
  • Chronic ear infections
  • Possible vomiting/diarrhea

Treatment

8-12 week elimination diet trial with prescription food. Antipruritics for symptom relief. Identify and avoid trigger ingredients.

Immune-Mediated Diseases Severe
Dog

Overview

Conditions where immune system attacks the body's own cells. Includes various forms of lupus, pemphigus, and other autoimmune disorders.

Symptoms

  • Varies by specific condition
  • Skin lesions, ulcers, crusting
  • Hair loss
  • Systemic illness in some cases

Treatment

Immunosuppressive medications like steroids, cyclosporine, or Apoquel. Often requires specialist dermatologist care.

Pyoderma Moderate
Dog

Overview

Bacterial skin infection - can be superficial (upper layers) or deep (more serious). Often secondary to other skin conditions.

Symptoms

  • Pustules, redness, irritation
  • Discharge and scabs (deep pyoderma)
  • Painful skin lesions
  • Possible systemic illness

Treatment

Antibiotics (often long-term), medicated baths, topical treatments. Address underlying causes.

Seborrhea Moderate
Dog

Overview

Skin disease causing defective keratinization, leading to excessive scaling. Can be primary (genetic) or secondary (caused by other conditions).

Symptoms

  • Dry flakes and scales (seborrhea sicca)
  • Greasy skin with oily scales (seborrhea oleosa)
  • Possible odor
  • Secondary infections

Treatment

Primary form is managed with medicated baths and topical treatments. Secondary form requires treating underlying condition.

Ticks Moderate
Dog

Overview

External parasites that attach to skin and feed on blood. Main concern is transmission of diseases like Lyme, Ehrlichia, and Rocky Mountain Spotted Fever.

Symptoms

  • Visible ticks attached to skin
  • Local skin irritation
  • Possible systemic illness from diseases
  • Anemia in severe infestations

Treatment

Proper tick removal. Use veterinarian-recommended tick control products. Monitor for signs of tick-borne diseases.
