Cat Disease Encyclopedia

Comprehensive guide to common cat illnesses and treatments

Kidney Disease Severe
Cat

Overview

Kidneys filter waste and regulate blood/water levels. Cats can develop acute renal failure (potentially reversible) or chronic kidney disease (manageable but not curable). Causes include age, infections, trauma, blockages, or poisoning.

Symptoms

  • Frequent urination
  • Weight loss
  • Excessive thirst
  • Dry coat
  • Vomiting and diarrhea

Treatment

Diagnosis requires bloodwork and urinalysis. Acute cases may be reversible with prompt treatment. Chronic cases require lifelong management with special diet and medications.

Dental Disease Moderate
Cat

Overview

50-90% of cats over 4 years suffer from dental disease. Main types are gingivitis, periodontitis, and tooth resorption. Can cause severe pain affecting eating and leading to malnutrition.

Types & Symptoms

  • Gingivitis: Red, swollen, bleeding gums
  • Periodontitis: Gum damage, halitosis, drooling
  • Tooth Resorption: Pain, head tilting while eating

Treatment

Professional dental cleaning, possible extractions. Daily tooth brushing helps prevent recurrence. Special diets may help maintain oral health.

Diabetes Severe
Cat

Overview

Caused by inability to produce or respond to insulin. Can result from genetics, pancreatic disease, or obesity. Serious but manageable, with some cats achieving remission.

Symptoms

  • Weight loss
  • Increased thirst/urination
  • Lethargy
  • Dehydration
  • Appetite changes

Treatment

Insulin therapy, dietary management, weight control. Regular monitoring of blood glucose levels. Some cats may go into remission with proper treatment.

Feline Immunodeficiency Virus (FIV) Severe
Cat

Overview

Compromises immune system, leaving cats vulnerable to infections. Spread mainly through bite wounds (outdoor male cats at highest risk). No cure but can be managed.

Symptoms

  • Gingivitis
  • Loss of appetite
  • Poor coat condition
  • Fever
  • Chronic/recurring infections

Treatment

Diagnosed through bloodwork. Management focuses on preventing secondary infections. Indoor lifestyle recommended. Can live normal lifespan with proper care.

Feline Leukemia Virus (FeLV) Critical
Cat

Overview

Cancer-causing retrovirus spread through saliva, blood, feces, or urine. Weakens immune system and causes severe anemia. Kittens often infected by mothers.

Symptoms

  • Seizures/neurological disorders
  • Eye conditions
  • Persistent diarrhea
  • Persistent fever
  • Recurrent infections
  • Weight loss and anorexia

Treatment

No cure exists. Prevention through vaccination is key. Infected cats need careful management to prevent disease progression and secondary infections.

Cancer Critical
Cat

Overview

Lymphoma is most common, but many forms exist. FIV/FeLV positive cats at higher risk. Siamese breeds more susceptible. Early detection improves outcomes.

Symptoms

  • Weight loss
  • Anorexia
  • Diarrhea
  • Vomiting
  • Visible lumps (sometimes)

Treatment

Depends on cancer type and stage. May include surgery, chemotherapy, or radiation. Regular vet exams crucial for early detection.

Obesity Moderate
Cat

Overview

Defined as 20% over ideal weight. Shortens lifespan and increases risk of diabetes, joint problems, cancer, and heart disease.

Symptoms

  • Excess body fat
  • Difficulty grooming
  • Lethargy
  • Labored breathing

Treatment

Veterinary-supervised weight loss plan. Gradual dietary changes and increased exercise. Avoid crash diets which can be dangerous.

Pancreatitis Severe
Cat

Overview

Inflammation of pancreas, often with liver/intestinal involvement. Cause unknown but cats with diabetes or IBD at higher risk.

Symptoms

  • Vomiting
  • Fever
  • Abdominal pain
  • Decreased appetite
  • Diarrhea

Treatment

Hospitalization often needed for IV fluids and medications. Pain management crucial. Long-term dietary changes may be recommended.

Hyperthyroidism Moderate
Cat

Overview

Endocrine disease in middle-aged/older cats caused by benign thyroid growths increasing hormone production.

Symptoms

  • Excessive energy
  • Increased appetite
  • Weight loss
  • Restlessness
  • Increased thirst/urination

Treatment

Diagnosed through blood tests. Options include medication, radioactive iodine therapy, surgery, or special diet. Treatment can restore normal thyroid function.

Broken Bones Severe
Cat

Overview

Occurs when excessive stress/pressure is applied to bone. Requires immediate veterinary attention to minimize long-term effects.

Symptoms

  • Limping or inability to use limb
  • Swelling at injury site
  • Pain when touched
  • Visible deformity (in severe cases)

Treatment

X-rays for diagnosis. May require splinting, casting, or surgery. Pain management and restricted activity during healing.
