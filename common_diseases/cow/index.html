Cow Disease Encyclopedia

Comprehensive guide to common cattle illnesses and treatments

Mastitis Severe
Cow

Overview

Inflammation of the udder tissue that causes significant economic losses in dairy production. Most common in high-producing dairy cows during early lactation.

Symptoms

  • Swollen, hot, painful udder
  • Abnormal milk (flakes, clots, watery)
  • Reduced milk yield
  • Reluctance to be milked
  • Fever in severe cases

Treatment

Intramammary antibiotics, anti-inflammatory drugs. Milk out affected quarters frequently. Improve milking hygiene and teat care. Isolate infected animals.

Bovine Spongiform Encephalopathy (BSE) Critical
Cow

Overview

Fatal neurodegenerative disease caused by prions. Affects the brain and spinal cord, causing sponge-like holes in brain tissue.

Symptoms

  • Behavioral changes (nervousness, aggression)
  • Difficulty standing/walking
  • Loss of coordination
  • Weight loss despite normal appetite
  • Progressive neurological deterioration

Treatment

No treatment available. Prevention through strict feed bans on ruminant-derived proteins. Mandatory reporting and culling of affected animals.

Calf Scour Severe
Calves

Overview

Leading cause of calf mortality worldwide. Can be caused by bacteria (E. coli, Salmonella), viruses (Rotavirus, Coronavirus), or parasites (Cryptosporidium).

Symptoms

  • Watery diarrhea (yellow, white or bloody)
  • Dehydration (sunken eyes, skin tenting)
  • Weakness and depression
  • Reduced suckling reflex
  • Rough hair coat

Treatment

Oral rehydration solutions, electrolytes. Antibiotics for bacterial causes. Ensure colostrum intake. Keep calves warm and dry. Isolate sick calves.

Foot Rot Moderate
Cattle

Overview

Bacterial infection (Fusobacterium necrophorum) of the hoof that thrives in wet, muddy conditions. Causes severe lameness if untreated.

Symptoms

  • Sudden lameness (often one leg)
  • Swelling between claws
  • Foul-smelling discharge
  • Fever in acute cases
  • Reluctance to bear weight

Treatment

Systemic antibiotics (penicillin, oxytetracycline). Foot baths with zinc sulfate or copper sulfate. Keep animals in dry areas. Regular hoof trimming.

Foot and Mouth Disease Critical
Cattle

Overview

Highly contagious viral disease affecting cloven-hoofed animals. Causes significant economic losses due to trade restrictions.

Symptoms

  • Fever (104-106Â°F)
  • Excessive drooling
  • Vesicles on tongue, gums, teats, hooves
  • Severe lameness
  • Drop in milk production

Treatment

No specific treatment. Vaccination in endemic areas. Strict quarantine measures. Report to authorities immediately if suspected.

Anthrax Critical
Cattle

Overview

Zoonotic disease caused by Bacillus anthracis spores that can survive in soil for decades. Often causes sudden death in grazing animals.

Symptoms

  • Sudden death (often first sign)
  • High fever before death
  • Staggering, difficulty breathing
  • Bloody discharge from natural openings
  • Rapid bloating after death

Treatment

Report immediately to authorities. Do NOT necropsy. Vaccination in endemic areas. Carcasses must be burned or buried deep with lime.

Bovine Abortion Severe
Cows

Overview

Premature termination of pregnancy with multiple potential causes including infectious agents, toxins, and nutritional deficiencies.

Symptoms

  • Vaginal discharge (often bloody)
  • Expulsion of fetus
  • Retained placenta
  • Decreased milk production
  • Metritis in some cases

Treatment

Identify and address underlying cause. Proper disposal of aborted materials. Vaccinate against common abortifacient diseases. Improve nutrition.

Actinomycosis (Lumpy Jaw) Moderate
Cattle

Overview

Chronic bacterial infection causing bony swellings of the jaw. Typically enters through mouth wounds from rough feed or sharp objects.

Symptoms

  • Hard, painless jaw swelling
  • Progressive enlargement
  • Difficulty chewing
  • Weight loss
  • Pus discharge in advanced cases

Treatment

Long-term antibiotics (penicillin, iodides). Surgical drainage may be needed. Remove sharp objects from feed. Poor prognosis in advanced cases.

Ephemeral Fever Moderate
Cattle

Overview

Viral disease transmitted by biting insects. Seasonal occurrence, often following heavy rains that increase insect populations.

Symptoms

  • Sudden high fever (104-107Â°F)
  • Muscle stiffness and lameness
  • Shivering and trembling
  • Nasal discharge
  • Recumbency in severe cases

Treatment

Supportive care (anti-inflammatories, fluids). Most animals recover in 3-5 days. Control insect vectors. Vaccine available in some countries.
